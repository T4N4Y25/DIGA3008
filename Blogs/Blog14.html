Justice and Ethics in programming practices

Justice and ethics become more of a essential topic within programming spaces as the world continues to transition to a digital space. As a programmer, I come to the realization that I have a lot of control within this digital space, and with such freedom I must also be more thoughtful as to what I put out there. Everything that gets put onto the web should match the criteria of being ethical, inclusive and sustainable (Okoye, 2024).

One approach to correctly incorporate justice and ethics is to have a good understanding of ethics themselves within digital spaces. From the course curriculum I know the various deceptive design patterns which is fundamental to avoid doing these design patterns within my own programs.

Inclusivity is also important to help move away from colonialism in the digital space. By representing my culture and identity as a South African citizen within my programs, I can bring a broader awareness to South Africa as a whole.

Key points which help in ethical programming are as follows: prioritize user’s safety and privacy, ensure there is no bias towards certain groups within the program, ensure the program is well optimized, make the program highly accessible to those with disabilities, remove any language barriers and show transparency to the user on how their data is being used (Okoye, 2024).

One emphasized point on design justice that I have frequently seen throughout the course is the concept of the digital divide and the consequences of this divide. Therefore, the digital divide should be something constantly thought of when creating a program. Due to the limited resources available in certain locations, the program should always be optimized to account for this, for example, the lack of access to mobile networks and Wi-Fi can make getting online very difficult; to counter this, an offline option can be available to minimize the need for ICT resources.

With all the benefits of the internet, there is also opportunity for malicious individuals to misuse the internet. When creating a program, security should be vital whenever a user’s personal information is used to prevent the risk of their information being misused by someone else. To help establish confidence in the program, it is also important to be transparent with the user to help reassure them that their data is being handled safely.
