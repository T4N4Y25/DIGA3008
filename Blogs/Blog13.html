Ethical Internet

Throughout the course for Interactive Media, I have engaged in discussions and readings regarding ethics towards the internet. My interaction with the course has broadened my understanding on what it means to have an ethical internet.

The introduction of the internet has brought with it many innovations towards our global economy by reducing economic friction regarding transport, communication and searching (Greenstein, 2018). The introduction of the internet has also brought about a greater separation of people through the digital divide in which unequal access to the internet has brought about a greater divide between regions and people. Additionally, the internet has made advancements in colonialism allowing power to shift towards certain regions or demographics. While the internet has had positive impacts on our society, the internet has unfortunately brought about its own set of problems which need to be addressed.

The digital divide is the ever-growing gap between those who have access to ICT services and those who do not (TechTarget, 2024). With the world constantly moving towards a more digital era, those who lack access to the required resources fall further behind. The internet has innovated how we view information, distribution of goods and services, and how we can connect with each other further emphasising the need to bridge the digital divide to allow everyone to integrate and benefit from the digital era.

Practices need to be assessed regarding decolonizing the internet. The internet should be a place where people from different regions and background can thoroughly express themselves without being forced through a standard 'template'. The internet and programming in general should be more inclusive and represent people and cultures from every region of the world to avoid western society as being seen as the "norm". Steps are being taken to promote this such as through the google search engine supporting translation of languages, windows displaying beautiful scenery from different countries as wallpapers, and language accessibility becoming more standard. These are steps in the right direction, and I hope to see more being done to bridge the gap between different cultures and people, therefore putting an end to colonialist mindsets for good.

To me an ethical internet is one where everyone has access to and can benefit from the web without restrictions based on location and economic state. An ethical internet is one that also promotes acceptance between different people from different backgrounds. The internet is a powerful resource and it should be used to help bridge the gap between people to create a truly unified world.
